What is CS50?

CS50 is harvard University's introduction to the intellectual enterprises of computer science and the art of programming for majors and non-majors alike, with or without prior programming experience. CS50x teaches students how to think algorithmically and solve problems efficiently. Topics include abstraction, algorithms, data structures, encapsulation, resource management, security, software engineering, and web development. Languages include C, Python, SQL, and JavaScript plus CSS and HTML.

It is worth it?

Yes, CS50 is definitely worth it. It is a highly acclaimed computer science course that provides a comprehensive understanding of the subject. With its engaging lectures, challenging problem sets, and supportive community, CS50 equips students with valuable skills and knowledge that are highly sought after in the tech industry. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced programmer, CS50 offers a rewarding learning experience that can significantly enhance your career prospects.

It is easy or hard?

The difficulty level of the CS50 course can vary depending on the individual. For some, especially those new to programming, it can be challenging due to the rigorous problem sets and the complex concepts covered. However, the course is designed to accommodate learners of all levels, and it provides extensive support through lectures, tutorials, and an active community. With dedication, perseverance, and the willingness to put in the effort, even beginners (like me) can succeed in CS50.

how much time it takes?

The amount of time it takes to complete CS50 can vary depending on various factors, including prior programming experience and the level of commitment you dedicate to the course. On average, you can expect to spend around 10-20 hours per week on CS50, which includes attending lectures, completing problem sets, and studying the material. The course is typically structured to be completed within a semester, spanning approximately 12 weeks. However, it's worth noting that some learners (like me) may take longer to complete the course based on their individual pace.

What I think about the course?

I believe it is a fantastic course, although I must admit that the beginning can be quite challenging for individuals without prior experience. Personally, I would recommend seeking additional learning resources outside the course if you find it difficult to understand initially. Take my own experience as an example: the beginning was incredibly tough, and I nearly gave up. However, I persevered and continued, and now I consider myself a fairly proficient programmer.
