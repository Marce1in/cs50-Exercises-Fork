Who I am?

Hey there! I'm Marcelo, an 18-year-old Brazilian developer who's simply looking for happiness. Coding is my passion (except for CSS, I'm not a big fan of that). Currently, I'm enrolled in two courses: CS50 and a SENAC course (a Brazilian university). Next year, I plan to attend college, although I haven't decided on the exact course just yet. However, I'm certain it will be related to computers. I hope you enjoyed this website; it was quite a remarkable challenge for me and taught me a lot about front-end development. It also made me realize that CSS isn't my cup of tea.

What are my experiences in coding?

The bulk of my coding experiences stem from my CS50 course. It has been an incredible journey where I've delved into various programming languages such as C, Python, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and even dabbled in mySQLITE. Along the way, I've gained valuable insights into how computers function and operate. In addition to CS50, I'm also enrolled in a SENAC course that primarily focuses on Python. While it may seem like a smaller course in comparison, it shouldn't be underestimated. It has taught me the importance of teamwork and provided me with a unique perspective on how others perceive coding. This knowledge has proven to be quite invaluable. As for professional experience, I'm still in the early stages of building my portfolio. However, I remain hopeful that one day I'll have the opportunity to apply my skills and gain practical work experience.

What are my dreams?

At the moment, I don't have a laundry list of grand, long-term dreams. Life seems to be zooming by faster than I anticipated. However, I do have a simple aspiration: to break free from being a slave to money throughout my entire life. Money shouldn't dictate every aspect of my existence. In the short term, my dreams revolve around exploring various programming areas and discovering the one that truly captures my heart. I also yearn to kick my habit of procrastinating on my projects, reignite my exercise routine, and engage in more meaningful conversations with people. Ultimately, my goal is to find happiness. Is that really too much to ask for?
